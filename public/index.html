The Framework

Before the
Commitment,
the Investigation.

The most expensive mistake in career preparation is committing significant time and money before knowing if the path is actually right. ALT makes investigation come first.

Start the Finder
01
Identify What Pulls You
Not a personality type. Not a test score. What actually interests you, and why? The Dispatch, the quiz, and the pathway stories are designed to surface real interests — not match you to whatever's popular.
02
Get a Firsthand View
Meet people who are already doing it. Job shadow programs, open houses, and mentorships through ACE and the trade organizations give you actual exposure before any commitment.
03
Eliminate What Doesn't Fit
Crossing a path off the list is not failure — it's progress. Narrow from many possibilities down to the few worth exploring deeply. That's the job of investigation.
04
Follow Up Before You Commit
Expert advisement. Workplace observation. The organizations in ALT can connect you with field contacts who answer the questions no website can. Then — and only then — commit time and money.

For Parents & Guardians

The Questions
You're
Actually
Asking

You want your kid to be secure. That's the whole thing. Here's what the research actually says — without the guilt trip about college, and without the oversell on trades.

Is a trade career actually stable long-term? +
Yes — and increasingly more so than many white-collar fields. AI is automating knowledge work faster than physical skilled trades. An electrician, HVAC tech, or plumber cannot be outsourced or replaced by software. Trade workers consistently out-earn most college graduates within a decade — with significantly less debt. Business ownership by years 6–8 compounds these numbers further.
What if my child changes their mind after starting? +
Most alternative pathways are designed for exactly this. A 6-month welding certification costs a fraction of one semester of college. An HVAC certification takes 18 months. The cost of changing direction is far lower than two years at a four-year university. ALT's framework: investigate first, commit second, treat exploration as the goal — not the failure.
How do I help my child figure out what they actually want? +
Get them exposure before asking for commitment. Job shadows, open house events at JCTC or IEC Kentuckiana, informational interviews with working professionals — these experiences are worth more than any personality test. ALT's Finder quiz is a starting point for conversation, not a verdict. Use it to open doors, not close them.
What about college — is it completely off the table? +
Not at all. ALT isn't anti-college. It's pro-intention. If your child has a specific, concrete reason for a four-year degree — a field that genuinely requires it, a clear career goal — then college makes complete sense. What ALT pushes back on is college as the default answer when no other options have been explored. The question isn't "college or not?" — it's "what path is actually right, and have we looked at all of them?"
How do I find a mentor in a specific field? +
ACE Mentor specifically connects high school students with working professionals in architecture, construction, and engineering at no cost. IBEW, IEC, and the KY Career Center all have direct contact programs. The Contribute section also lets you submit a request for specific field connections — we'll route it to the right people.
